# Chapter 1. Introduction

## 1.1 Context and Motivation

In today's era of Industry 4.0, when the world continues to develop and change under the rapid advancement of digital technologies, people have gained access to a wide range of modern technological solutions across various fields such as healthcare, economics, and entertainment. These technological innovations have significantly contributed to improving quality of life while reducing the pressure and workload in many professional areas. As a result, technology has increasingly affirmed its vital role in modern society, especially in the field of event management and entertainment, where accuracy, speed, and flexibility are essential for effective organization and ticket distribution.

As of now, Vietnam has a rapidly growing entertainment and events industry, accommodating a diverse range of activities from music concerts and theater performances to large-scale sports tournaments. Each major event attracts thousands of attendees from different provinces across the country, as well as international tourists. The continuous growth in audience numbers has led to an increasingly large and complex volume of information that must be managed and processed instantly. This data not only includes personal customer information but also real-time seating availability, payment statuses, and strict access control records.

Therefore, managing and selling tickets using traditional manual methods or outdated platforms is no longer feasible and cannot meet current requirements in terms of speed, accuracy, and flexibility. Moreover, the process of handling massive concurrent booking requests and aggregating data for important purposes such as revenue reporting and statistical analysis faces many limitations, which in turn reduces the effectiveness of overall event management.

From the customers' perspective, each attendee has a strong need to seamlessly access information about event schedules, interactive seat selections, and official announcements from the organizers. Without a dedicated, highly responsive ticketing system, customers may encounter significant difficulties in securing tickets fairly and obtaining timely updates, leading to frustration and poor user experiences.

Based on the practical objectives mentioned above, the development of a modern, distributed event ticketing system is both necessary and appropriate in the era of Industry 4.0. Such a system not only helps event administrative staff and organizers improve operational efficiency but also enables customers to be more proactive and secure in their booking process. Consequently, it contributes to the establishment of a modern, professional, and sustainable entertainment ecosystem.

This system not only helps reduce the amount of administrative workload but also makes event management more efficient and improves the overall user environment. Additionally, it integrates useful features such as real-time updates and specialized functions for different user roles. Imagine a sweltering afternoon at a popular concert venue's ticket booth more than a decade ago. The familiar sight back then was staff members buried under stacks of printed ticket booklets taller than themselves, frantically crossing out seat numbers on a giant physical map with edges worn thin by time. To verify a returning customer's reservation or process a group booking, one had to spend hours searching through dusty ledgers, plagued by the constant anxiety of misplacing information or accidentally overselling a seat.

The struggle was not one-sided. For event-goers, securing a ticket to a highly anticipated show was often an exhausting, passive "wait-and-see" journey. Critical announcements regarding ticket sales were pinned to physical bulletin boards or printed on flyers, where information could easily be missed or fail to reach the right person in time. Fans would queue for hours outside venues, unaware of actual ticket availability. A single minor error in manual data entry or communication could lead to significant consequences, such as double-booked seats and chaotic check-in experiences at the venue gates.
